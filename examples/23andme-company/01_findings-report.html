Panel 01

The Thesis

For curious individuals who want to understand their genetic makeup, 23andMe is a D2C DNA kit. Unlike clinical testing ordered by a physician, it is cheap, consumer-friendly, and accessible directly via mail, bypassing the medical establishment.

Panel 02

The Market

The consumer genetics market does not exist. The B2B genetics market (research, clinical) is massive but opaque. We are creating a new TAM by dropping the price of genotyping below $99 and making it a consumer product.

Panel 03

The Winners

Analogues include Ancestry.com (proved consumers will pay for genealogical history) and early D2C health disruptors like WebMD (proved consumers want direct access to health information).

Panel 04

The Playbook

  1. The Laboratory Partnership: Don't build a massive sequencing lab. Partner with an existing CLIA-certified lab to handle the physical processing.
  2. The Ancestry Wedge: Lead with fun, low-stakes data (ancestry, traits like "cilantro tastes like soap") to avoid FDA crackdowns.
  3. The Database Moat: Ask users to opt-in to research. The real value of the company is owning the world's largest phenotypic and genotypic database.
Panel 05

The Graveyard

Early D2C Diagnostic startups: Killed by the FDA for making unsubstantiated medical claims or failing to secure proper lab certifications.

Panel 08

The Org

This is a biotech startup masquerading as a tech startup. We cannot lead with software engineering. We must lead with Clinical/Regulatory compliance, otherwise the government will literally shut us down.

Panel 09

The Call

Verdict: BUILD.

The regulatory moat is the hardest part. If we can solve the FDA compliance puzzle, we will have a monopoly on consumer genetic data. We proceed by securing legal counsel and a lab partner.
