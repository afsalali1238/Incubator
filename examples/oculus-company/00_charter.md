# Oculus VR — Company Charter

**Project:** Oculus VR. A consumer hardware company building an affordable, wide-field-of-view virtual reality headset using commodity smartphone parts.
**CEO Verdict:** BUILD. The incumbent VR industry is stuck building $100,000 enterprise rigs. Mobile screen tech has advanced enough to make a $300 consumer headset possible.
**Riskiest Assumption:** Tracking latency can be reduced below 20ms. If it's higher, players will experience severe motion sickness, killing consumer adoption instantly.
**The Wedge:** Building a "Developer Kit" (DK1) out of duct-taped parts to sell to hardcore game developers on Kickstarter, proving the market exists before attempting mass consumer manufacturing.
**The Scope Fence:** We are NOT building our own games or software platform yet. We are purely building the hardware peripheral.

## Org at a Glance
1. **Optical / Sensor Engineer** (Idea/MVP) — to solve the latency and tracking math.
2. **Hardware Hacker / Prototyper** (MVP) — to physically build the duct-taped DK1.
3. **Developer Relations Lead** (Launch) — to convince John Carmack and other devs to build games for a duct-taped prototype.

## Next Action
Assemble the first functional prototype using a disassembled smartphone screen, ski goggles, and an IMU sensor, and measure the motion-to-photon latency.
