01 The Thesis

Terrarium Planet

For casual strategy players who find deep simulation games overwhelming, Terrarium Planet is a god-sim game that limits interactions to simple elemental placements. The riskiest assumption is that simple placements produce enough emergent complexity to remain engaging after the first hour.

02 The Winners

Analogues that worked

Townscaper: Nailed the "zen" placement without management. Their wedge was simply placing blocks on water and watching houses auto-generate.

Doodle God: The combinatorial element matrix (A + B = C) was the entire game loop. It lacked spatial simulation, which Terrarium Planet solves.

08 The Org

Hiring the founding team

  • Systems Designer: De-risks the emergent elemental matrix.
  • Engine Specialist: De-risks the 2D grid performance for thousands of cells.
  • UX/UI Lead: De-risks the interaction model.
