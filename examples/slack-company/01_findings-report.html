Panel 01

The Thesis

For modern software and design teams drowning in internal email, Slack is a team communication tool that centralizes knowledge. Unlike Skype or email, it organizes conversations into persistent channels and makes every file and message instantly searchable.

Panel 02

The Market

The enterprise collaboration market is massive but stagnant. Tools are bought by IT departments, not end-users. The opportunity is a "bottom-up" adoption model where a team starts using it for free and forces IT to pay for it later.

Panel 03

The Winners

Analogues include Yammer (proved enterprise social is viable but execution is clunky) and Campfire by 37signals (proved persistent chat works but lacks deep integrations).

Panel 04

The Playbook

  1. The Polish: Take a nerdy protocol (IRC) and put a consumer-grade, joyful UI on it.
  2. The Integrations: Make it the central hub by integrating with tools devs already use (GitHub, Jira).
  3. The Bottom-Up Wedge: Make it free for small teams so they adopt it without asking permission.
Panel 08

The Org

To win this, we don't need enterprise salespeople yet. We need a Product/UX Designer. The entire moat is that the product feels so good to use that teams refuse to go back to email.

Panel 09

The Call

Verdict: PIVOT AND BUILD.

The game failed, but the tool is validated by our own intense daily usage. The risk is Go-To-Market against legacy enterprise software. We will bypass IT entirely by focusing on end-user joy.
