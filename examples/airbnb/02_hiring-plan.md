# Hiring Plan — Airbnb
CEO verdict: BUILD
Org template: Creator Marketplace
Time budget: Full-time

---

## Founding Roster (Day Zero → MVP)

### Tier 1 — Hire now (de-risks the riskiest assumption)

**1. Community & Supply Lead**
- Why first: The entire marketplace collapses without hosts. Demand is useless without supply. This role proves the riskiest assumption — that strangers will open their homes.
- De-risks: "Homeowners will let strangers sleep in their homes"
- Skills: host acquisition, listing quality, city launch playbook, supply-demand theory
- Hire trigger: Day 1
- Exit criterion: 3 SF hosts live and fully booked for IDSA conference

**2. Trust & Safety Designer**
- Why second: Without a mutual-review system and basic payment escrow, no guest books and no host repeats. Trust infrastructure is what makes a peer marketplace different from Craigslist.
- De-risks: "Guests and hosts will trust a stranger enough to transact"
- Skills: review system design, identity frameworks, payment escrow logic, host/guest policy
- Hire trigger: Day 1 (alongside Supply Lead)
- Exit criterion: v1 review flow live, first 3 transactions completed without incident

---

### Tier 2 — Hire after IDSA validation

**3. Product Engineer**
- Why third: Only hire after the manual test proves the concept. Building a self-serve listing flow before you know hosts will repeat is waste.
- De-risks: "We can build self-serve fast enough to scale beyond manual onboarding"
- Skills: frontend (mobile-first), booking system, listing flow, search/filter
- Hire trigger: Post-IDSA — at least 2 of 3 hosts say they'd do it again
- Exit criterion: Founder-independent listing creation; first booking completed without CEO involvement

**4. Growth & Demand Lead**
- Why fourth: Demand-side acquisition is worthless without supply. Build supply density first, then pull demand in.
- De-risks: "We can acquire guests at a CAC lower than booking margin"
- Skills: SEO for event/neighborhood demand, referral mechanics, PR, demand-side activation
- Hire trigger: Verified listings in 3+ cities, self-serve listing live
- Exit criterion: First organic (non-founder-sourced) bookings in each city

---

### Tier 3 — Hire at Launch / PMF signal

**5. Brand & Content Lead**
- Why fifth: Brand voice becomes critical when you scale beyond founder storytelling. "Belong anywhere" positioning is a competitive moat — but only after product-market fit is confirmed.
- De-risks: "We can build a brand that makes strangers feel safe enough to book"
- Skills: host storytelling, community voice, content strategy, positioning
- Hire trigger: "Very disappointed" PMF survey score >40%
- Exit criterion: Consistent host stories published; measurable uplift in new host applications from content

---

## Sequencing rationale

The riskiest thing about Airbnb is not technology — it is human trust. The first two hires attack trust from both sides simultaneously: supply (will homeowners open their homes?) and safety (will guests feel secure enough to book?). Everything else — product, growth, brand — is contingent on that being true.

Do NOT hire the Product Engineer until the manual test runs. eBay listed items manually before building seller tools. Airbnb should do the same.
