Problem
Travelers are priced out of hotels during demand spikes (sold-out events, conferences). Short-term alternative accommodations don't exist at scale — VRBO/HomeAway require week+ minimums.
Why Now
Craigslist proved strangers transact online. Facebook normalized identity. SF design community is tight-knit and trust-native. IDSA conference in 3 weeks — hotels already sold out.
Solution
Peer-to-peer short-stay marketplace. Homeowners list spare rooms. Travelers book by the night. Airbed + breakfast — cheap, personal, better than a sold-out hotel.
Wedge
IDSA Design Conference, San Francisco — August 2008. 3 hosts, all booked, zero hotel alternatives. Proof of concept in 1 event.
Riskiest Assumption
Homeowners will let strangers sleep in their homes. If this fails, the entire supply side evaporates and the marketplace has nothing to sell.
Cheap Test
3 hosts. 1 conference. Sell out all 3. If guests check out and hosts are willing to do it again — thesis validated in 72 hours.
Scope Fence (v1)
✅ SF only  ·  ✅ Event-overflow demand only  ·  ✅ Spare rooms / airbeds only
❌ Not long-term rental  ·  ❌ Not hotel aggregator  ·  ❌ Not VRBO competitor
Org Template
Creator Marketplace — community-led growth, supply quality gating, host identity before scale
Phase 2 Research Note
Winners: Couchsurfing (trust without money) + eBay (strangers transact). Graveyard: Wimdu (copied without trust layer). Moat: supply density + review flywheel.
