# Charter — Airbnb

**Project:** Airbnb (working title: AirBed & Breakfast)
**CEO verdict:** BUILD

---

## The Thesis

For urban travelers priced out of sold-out hotels during high-demand events, and for city dwellers with spare rooms sitting empty, Airbnb is a peer-to-peer home-sharing marketplace that turns unused residential space into affordable short-term accommodation. Unlike hotels (impersonal, expensive, limited inventory) or Craigslist (unstructured, no trust, no payment rails), it combines verified host profiles, upfront pricing, and payment escrow with a mutual-review system that makes strangers trust each other. The riskiest assumption is that homeowners will let strangers sleep in their homes. We'll test it by recruiting 3 hosts for the IDSA design conference in San Francisco — hotels are sold out, 1,000 attendees need somewhere to stay. If we book all three, the supply-side fear is solvable. Time budget: full-time (both founders committed).

---

## The Riskiest Assumption

Homeowners will let strangers sleep in their homes if there is a trust layer (verified profiles + mutual reviews + payment escrow). Cheap test: 3 hosts, 1 conference, sold out — does anyone actually book, and does anything go wrong?

---

## The Wedge

Conference overflow. Hotels sell out around major events; there is stranded demand with no good option. This is the first use case — not "travel in general." Lock in the conference niche first, prove the trust model works, then expand to general travel.

---

## The Scope Fence

Not a long-term rental marketplace (that's Craigslist / Zillow). Not a hotel aggregator (that's Booking.com). Not a property management tool. Not a vacation rental for holiday homes (that's VRBO). Home-sharing only, short-term, event-overflow first. One city (San Francisco) in v1. No corporate travel. No shared dormitories. Entire homes and private rooms only.

---
