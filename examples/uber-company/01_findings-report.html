Panel 01

The Thesis

For tech workers in San Francisco who cannot reliably get a taxi, UberCab is an SMS-based dispatch service that connects you instantly to a luxury black car. Unlike the taxi medallion cartel, it utilizes the idle downtime of licensed limo drivers to provide an on-demand, premium experience.

Panel 02

The Market

The TAM for "black car services" is small, but the TAM for "reliable urban transport" is massive. The market is artificially constrained by taxi medallions. We are expanding the market by making a premium service accessible on-demand.

Panel 03

The Winners

Analogues include Seamless/Grubhub (proving on-demand dispatch for restaurants works) and traditional taxi dispatchers (who use radio instead of smartphones).

Panel 04

The Playbook

  1. The Supply Side First: Build the supply of drivers before getting riders. A rider opening the app to zero cars will never open it again.
  2. The Premium Wedge: Start with high-end black cars so the initial user experience feels like a superpower (push a button, get a personal driver).
  3. The Hardware Hack: Drivers don't have smartphones yet. Buy iPhones, load them with the driver app, and physically hand them to the drivers.
Panel 08

The Org

We do not need a massive engineering team yet. We need a City General Manager. This is a physical, operational business that requires hand-to-hand combat to recruit drivers and deal with city transport regulators.

Panel 09

The Call

Verdict: BUILD.

The regulatory risk is massive, but the consumer pain point in SF is so high that riders will adopt it instantly if we can solve the supply side. We proceed by recruiting 5 drivers manually.
